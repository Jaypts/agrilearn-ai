# AgriLearn AI — Demo Video Script (5:00 limit)

**Format note:** This is written for ~5 minutes total. Feature-tour sections (2:50–4:35) are intentionally fast-paced — don't linger, quick screen cuts. Your live diagnosis demo and the real farmer footage are the two sections judges will remember most; give those the most room. If you're running long, trim from the fast-paced middle section first, never from the live demo or farmer footage.

Timestamps are guides, not hard cuts — adjust to your actual pacing once you record.

---

## [0:00–0:25] Cold open — the problem

*(You on camera, or voiceover over B-roll of a farm/leaf)*

> "In Uganda, one agricultural extension officer often serves over a thousand farmers. If your crop gets sick, by the time anyone trained can look at it, it's already spread. But almost every farmer now has something an extension officer doesn't have time to be: a camera in their pocket. This is AgriLearn AI."

## [0:25–0:45] What it is, fast

> "It's a phone-based plant doctor — built around one idea: don't just tell a farmer what's wrong, teach them why it happened, using their real farm's conditions, not a generic guess."

## [0:45–1:05] My Farm + live weather (quick)

*(Show the My Farm profile screen briefly, then the weather card)*

> "It starts by learning about your actual farm — district, crops, farm size. That unlocks something most tools don't have: live weather for your exact location, feeding directly into every diagnosis — temperature, humidity, even soil moisture and UV index, pulled from a real weather service in real time."

## [1:05–2:35] Live demo — the core moment, take your time here

*(Open the app, Diagnose tab — camera viewfinder already live)*

> "This is a live scanner, not a file picker."

*(Point at a leaf, wait for "🌿 Leaf detected")*

> "It's watching the feed in real time — see that, it found leaf tissue before I even captured."

*(Tap capture)*

> "Before this photo even reaches any AI, the app runs its own pixel-color analysis right here on the phone — no internet needed for this part."

*(Point at the green/yellow/brown bar)*

> "That's a real measurement of leaf damage, computed locally."

*(Tap Diagnose, wait for result)*

> "And here's the diagnosis. But look — it doesn't just give me an answer. It shows me what it actually saw in the photo, and what else it considered and ruled out — real differential diagnosis, the way a plant pathologist would reason, not a single guess."

*(Scroll to biology explanation + weather relevance section)*

> "This is the part I care about most — it explains *why* the disease happens. And because it knows today's weather on my actual farm, it can tell me whether current conditions make this worse."

*(Show quiz)*

> "Then it tests whether I actually understood that — not just whether I can repeat a name."

## [2:35–2:55] Real farmer reaction — your footage goes here

> "We didn't just want to demo this to judges — we tested it with [name], an actual farmer."

*(Insert your real footage/reaction here — let it run mostly unedited; a genuine reaction is worth more than any script)*

## [2:55–3:15] Soil Check — fast

*(Switch to Soil Check tab, show a real soil photo being analyzed)*

> "We also built a soil check — real AI analysis, not a gimmick. It even checks that you actually photographed soil before giving any result, and combines that with the same hand-feel test extension officers teach in the field."

## [3:15–3:40] Learn Library — fast

*(Switch to Learn Library, show a lesson with its questions)*

> "Every crop has a real course behind it — five lessons, each with real content and multiple questions, not just one quiz. You have to actually understand it to unlock the next lesson."

## [3:40–4:00] Field Journal — fast

*(Switch to Field Journal, expand an entry showing notes + follow-up status)*

> "Every diagnosis gets logged — and farmers can track what happened after: did the treatment work, is it resolved, did it come back. That's real data, not a static log."

## [4:00–4:20] Outbreak Map — the vision moment

*(Switch to Outbreak Map tab)*

> "Here's where this goes next. This is sample data today — we're upfront about that on screen — but it's built on the same logging architecture. If farmers across Uganda used this and opted in, this is what early disease-outbreak detection at a district level could look like, before it's visible to the naked eye in a neighboring field."

## [4:20–4:40] Our engineering approach

*(Switch to About tab)*

> "Here's a design choice we made deliberately. Instead of a narrow classifier trained on a fixed list of diseases, we built this on a reasoning model cross-checked against two signals we compute ourselves — pixel-level analysis and live weather data. That's what lets it flag low-confidence results honestly instead of guessing. The next step on our roadmap is validation with a trained agronomist — and we think being upfront about that now is part of building something farmers can actually trust."

## [4:40–5:00] Close

> "AgriLearn AI: built in Uganda, for Ugandan farmers, by someone who's watched this problem up close. Thank you."
